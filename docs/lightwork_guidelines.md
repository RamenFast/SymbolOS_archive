## Definition 🟡 #FADA5E — highest reason
**Lightwork** refers to low-stakes, high-frequency interactions that maintain system health, provide quick status updates, or perform minor adjustments without requiring the full cognitive overhead of a "Deepwork" session. It's the gentle breeze that keeps the windmill turning, the single line of code that prevents a cascade failure.

## Principles 🔴 #FF2400 — righteous boundary

- **Minimalism**: Use the smallest possible interaction to achieve the goal. Think of it as a single, perfectly placed domino.
- **Speed**: Prioritize low latency and quick completion. A stitch in time saves nine, and a quick commit saves a whole afternoon.
- **Safety**: Lightwork should never involve high-risk state changes without a transition to Deepwork. You wouldn't use a butter knife to slay a dragon, would you?
- **Transparency**: Even light actions must be auditable (provenance). Every whisper leaves a trace.

### 2. Minor Alignment 🧬 🟢 #228B22 — adaptability
- Fixing a typo in a doc.
- Updating a single tag in `symbol_map.shared.json`.
- Marking a small task as complete in `open_loops.md`.

### 3. Quick Capture 🧾 🧠 #E49B0F — higher intellect
- Adding a single "Seed note" to `future_possibilities_ring0.md`.
- Recording a quick decision in `decisions.md`.
- Appending a brief observation to the `session_log`.

## Transitioning to Deepwork 🟠 #FF8C00 — ambition
If a Lightwork task reveals a complex dependency, a significant safety risk, or requires deep architectural thought, it MUST be promoted to a Deepwork session.

- **Signal**: "This will take more than 5 minutes" or "I need to read 3+ files to understand this."
- **Action**: Create an Open Loop, update the Working Set, and schedule a Deepwork session.
