## Definition
**Lightwork** refers to low-stakes, high-frequency interactions that maintain system health, provide quick status updates, or perform minor adjustments without requiring the full cognitive overhead of a "Deepwork" session.

## Principles
- **Minimalism**: Use the smallest possible interaction to achieve the goal.
- **Speed**: Prioritize low latency and quick completion.
- **Safety**: Lightwork should never involve high-risk state changes without a transition to Deepwork.
- **Transparency**: Even light actions must be auditable (provenance).

## Lightwork Patterns

### 1. Status Checks 🕯️
- Running `mercer_status.ps1` to check for symbol drift.
- Skimming the `working_set.md` to re-orient.
- Checking the `shadow_queue` for pending precog suggestions.

### 2. Minor Alignment 🧬
- Fixing a typo in a doc.
- Updating a single tag in `symbol_map.shared.json`.
- Marking a small task as complete in `open_loops.md`.

### 3. Quick Capture 🧾
- Adding a single "Seed note" to `future_possibilities_ring0.md`.
- Recording a quick decision in `decisions.md`.
- Appending a brief observation to the `session_log`.

## Transitioning to Deepwork
If a Lightwork task reveals a complex dependency, a significant safety risk, or requires deep architectural thought, it MUST be promoted to a Deepwork session.

- **Signal**: "This will take more than 5 minutes" or "I need to read 3+ files to understand this."
- **Action**: Create an Open Loop, update the Working Set, and schedule a Deepwork session.

## DND and Lightwork
Lightwork is the preferred mode when DND is ON but the user still needs to maintain minimal system awareness.
